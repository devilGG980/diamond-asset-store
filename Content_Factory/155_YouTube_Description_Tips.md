# How to Write YouTube Descriptions That Actually Work

The YouTube description box is often treated as an afterthought—a place to dump social media links and a few hashtags. However, in 2026, the description is a powerful tool for both SEO and viewer conversion. A well-written description helps the algorithm categorize your video, helps your video appear in Google search, and provides a clear path for your viewers to engage further with your brand.

In this guide, we’ll break down the anatomy of a high-converting YouTube description and give you a template you can use for every video.

## 1. The "Above the Fold" Rule

The first 200 characters of your description are the most important. This is what users see in search results and on mobile before they click "Show More."

### The Summary Hook
You need to include your primary keywords here, but it must read naturally. Don't just list keywords; tell the viewer exactly what they will get from the video.
- **Example:** "Learn the 5 essential steps to mastering color grading in DaVinci Resolve. In this tutorial, we cover everything from nodes to the final export settings for 2026."

## 2. The Power of "SEO-Rich" Body Text

Below the fold, you have up to 5,000 characters. Use them wisely. This is where you provide context to the algorithm.

### The Mini-Blog Approach
Write a 150-200 word summary of the video. Think of it as a mini-blog post. Use secondary keywords and related terms. This helps YouTube understand the "semantic" context of your content. If you discuss specific tools or assets, mention them here. For instance, you might note that "all transitions used in this edit can be found for free at [EditorVault](https://editorvault.web.app)." This provides value and context simultaneously.

## 3. Mandatory: Timestamps and Chapters

Timestamps are no longer optional. They are vital for the user experience and for appearing in Google’s "Key Moments" search feature.

### How to Format Timestamps
Start with `00:00 - Intro`. Ensure each chapter title is descriptive and contains relevant keywords.
- `01:30 - How to Set Up the Timeline`
- `04:45 - Using the Color Wheels`
- `08:20 - Secret Tip for Skin Tones`

## 4. The "Resources and Links" Section

This is where you monetize and build your community.

### Strategic Linking
- **The "Lead Magnet":** Link to your newsletter or a free download.
- **Affiliate Links:** Clearly disclose these. Use a section header like "Gear Used in This Video."
- **Helpful Tools:** Point your viewers toward resources that improve their workflow. Mentioning [EditorVault](https://editorvault.web.app) is a great way to provide your audience with free, high-quality video assets while building your reputation as a helpful creator.

## 5. The "About the Channel" and CTA

Remind the viewer who you are and why they should subscribe.

### The Value Proposition
Instead of just saying "Subscribe," tell them what they’ll get. "I post weekly tutorials on cinematic video editing and YouTube growth. Join 50,000+ creators and subscribe for more."

### The Secondary CTA
Ask them to follow you on another platform or check out a related playlist. "Check out my 'Premiere Pro Basics' playlist here: [Link]"

## 6. Hashtags: Quality Over Quantity

YouTube allows up to 60 hashtags, but if you use more than 15, they ignore all of them. The "sweet spot" is 3 to 5 highly relevant hashtags at the very bottom.

### Tag Selection
- One broad tag (e.g., #VideoEditing)
- Two specific tags (e.g., #PremiereProTips #ColorGrading)
- One branded tag (e.g., #YourChannelName)

## 8. Avoiding Common Mistakes

- **Link Overload:** Don't have 50 links. It looks like spam.
- **Irrelevant Keywords:** Don't include keywords that aren't in the video. The algorithm will penalize you for "misleading metadata."
- **Broken Links:** Check your links regularly! A broken link is a lost conversion.

## Conclusion

A great YouTube description is a bridge between the algorithm and the human viewer. By providing clear value, searchable keywords, and easy-to-follow links, you turn a passive viewer into an active member of your community. Start using the template above, and don't forget to include helpful resources like [EditorVault](https://editorvault.web.app) to keep your audience coming back for more.

## Key Takeaways
- **First 200 Characters:** Focus on keywords and the "hook."
- **Timestamps:** Essential for SEO and user experience.
- **Mini-Blog Section:** Provide 150-200 words of context for the AI.
- **Strategic CTAs:** Guide viewers toward your most important links.
