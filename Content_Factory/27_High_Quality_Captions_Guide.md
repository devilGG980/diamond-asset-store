## 2. Auto-Captioning: The Modern Workflow

In the past, captioning was a tedious manual process. In 2026, AI has almost entirely automated the transcription phase.

### Premiere Pro: Text-Based Editing
Adobe has integrated "Speech-to-Text" directly into Premiere Pro. 
1.  Go to the **"Text"** panel and click **"Transcribe Sequence."**
2.  Once the transcription is done, click **"Create Captions."**
3.  Premiere will automatically generate a new "Subtitle" track that is perfectly synced to your audio.

### CapCut: The Mobile King
For TikTok and Reels creators, CapCut's "Auto-Captions" feature remains the fastest and most popular tool. It offers a variety of stylized, animated templates that are specifically designed for high-energy social media content.

## 3. Styling Your Captions for Maximum Impact

Standard "movie subtitles" (small, white text at the bottom) are often too boring for social media. To keep viewers engaged, you need to style your captions.

### The "Sweet Spot" for Placement
Never put captions at the very bottom or very top of the frame. On TikTok and Reels, the bottom is covered by the username and description, and the top is covered by the "Following/For You" tabs. Keep your captions in the **Middle Third** of the frame.

### Font Choice
Use bold, sans-serif fonts that are easy to read even on small screens. Fonts like **The Bold Font**, **Montserrat**, **Inter**, and **Outfit** are excellent choices. Avoid "curly" or thin fonts that disappear against a busy background.

### Adding a "Stroke" or "Shadow"
To ensure your text is readable regardless of what is happening in the video, add a subtle black stroke (outline) or a drop shadow. This provides contrast against bright backgrounds. ## 4. The "Hormozi" Style: Dynamic Captions

The "dynamic" caption style involves showing only 1-3 words at a time, often in different colors.

### Why It Works
By showing only a few words at once, you force the viewer to "read" the video. This increases focus and prevents them from scrolling past. 

### How to Do It:
*   Highlight key words (like "MONEY," "FREE," or "DANGER") in a different color (Yellow or Green).
*   Add a small "Pop" or "Scale" animation every time a new word appears.

## 5. Burned-In vs. Closed Captions

### Closed Captions (CC)
These are "toggable." The user can turn them on or off (like on YouTube). 
*   **Pros:** Better for SEO because the platform can "read" the text.
*   **Cons:** You have no control over the font or styling.

### Burned-In (Open) Captions
These are part of the video file itself. 
*   **Pros:** You control exactly how they look and where they are placed. They are guaranteed to show up on every platform.
*   **Cons:** They cannot be turned off and don't contribute directly to the platformâ€™s text-based SEO.

**Our Recommendation:** For long-form YouTube videos, use Closed Captions. For short-form social media (TikTok/Reels), always use Burned-In captions.

## 6. Pro Tip: Using Captions for SEO

Even if you use "Burned-In" captions, you should still upload a transcription file (.srt) to platforms like YouTube. This tells the algorithm exactly what your video is about, which helps you rank for specific keywords.
