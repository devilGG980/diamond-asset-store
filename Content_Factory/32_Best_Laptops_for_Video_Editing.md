# Top 5 Best Laptops for Video Editing Under $1,500 (2026)

In the past, video editing required a massive desktop computer with a noisy fan and a tangled mess of wires. In 2026, thanks to the massive leaps in mobile chip technology (like Apple’s M-series and NVIDIA’s RTX laptop GPUs), you can edit 4K video from a coffee shop, a train, or your couch.

However, the laptop market is crowded with "gaming" machines and "business" ultra-books. Which ones are actually built for the sustained heavy loads of video rendering? 

We’ve researched and tested the market to find the top 5 best laptops for video editing that cost under $1,500.

## 1. The Best Overall: MacBook Air (M3 Chip, 16GB RAM)

The MacBook Air has transitioned from a basic "student" laptop to a genuine creative powerhouse. With the M3 chip, it can handle 4K ProRes editing with ease, and it does so without a single fan.

*   **Why it’s great:** The M3’s "Media Engine" is specifically designed for video encoding and decoding. The screen is color-accurate (P3 wide color gamut), and the battery life is industry-leading.
*   **The Catch:** You **must** upgrade to at least 16GB of RAM. The base 8GB model will struggle with complex timelines.
*   **Price:** Around $1,299 - $1,499 (depending on storage).

## 2. The Best Windows Performance: ASUS ROG Zephyrus G14

If you prefer Windows or use software like DaVinci Resolve (which loves a dedicated GPU), the G14 is the perfect balance of portability and power.

*   **Why it’s great:** It features a dedicated NVIDIA RTX 40-series GPU, which is essential for GPU-accelerated effects and AI features. The 14-inch OLED display is arguably the best-looking screen in this price bracket.
*   **The Catch:** It runs hotter than the Mac and the fans can be loud during a long render.
*   **Price:** Often found on sale for $1,100 - $1,400.

## 3. The Best Screen for the Money: ASUS ProArt Studiobook 16

ASUS designed the ProArt line specifically for creators, not gamers. This means the money went into the screen and the build quality rather than RGB lights.

*   **Why it’s great:** It features a 16-inch 4K OLED display that is factory-calibrated for color accuracy. It also includes the "ASUS Dial"—a physical wheel integrated into the trackpad that lets you scrub your timeline or change brush sizes in Photoshop.
*   **The Catch:** It’s larger and heavier than the other options on this list.
*   **Price:** Around $1,399 for the base creator specs.

## 4. The Budget King: Acer Swift X 14

If you want the most "raw power" for every dollar spent, Acer is hard to beat. The Swift X packs a dedicated GPU and a great screen into a chassis that costs significantly less than the big-name brands.

*   **Why it’s great:** It often comes with 32GB of RAM and an RTX 4050 for under $1,200. This is the "sweet spot" for 4K video editing without breaking the bank.
*   **The Catch:** The build quality is mostly plastic, and the trackpad isn't as smooth as a MacBook or ASUS.
*   **Price:** $1,000 - $1,200.

## 5. The Best 2-in-1 for Creators: Lenovo Yoga Pro 7i

For those who also do photo editing or like to use a stylus for masking and rotoscoping, the Yoga Pro 7i is a versatile workhorse.

*   **Why it’s great:** It features a gorgeous 3K PureSight Pro display and a very high-quality keyboard. The Intel Core Ultra processors in the 2026 model include a dedicated NPU (Neural Processing Unit) to speed up AI tasks in Premiere Pro.
*   **The Catch:** The battery life drops significantly when you are doing heavy video rendering.
*   **Price:** $1,250 - $1,450.

## What to Look for in a Video Editing Laptop

If you decide to look at other models, ensure they meet these minimum "Creator Specs":

1.  **RAM:** 16GB is the absolute minimum. 32GB is preferred for 4K.
2.  **GPU:** Look for a dedicated NVIDIA RTX card (4050 or higher) for Windows, or an M2/M3 chip for Mac.
3.  **Display:** Look for "100% sRGB" or "DCI-P3" coverage. If the screen has poor color, your videos will look different on every other device.
4.  **Storage:** 512GB is fine for apps, but you’ll want a fast external SSD for your actual footage.
