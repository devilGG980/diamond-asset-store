# How to Claim Your Custom YouTube URL in 2026

When you first create a YouTube channel, your URL is a long, ugly string of random letters and numbers (e.g., `youtube.com/channel/UC123abc...`). This is impossible to remember and looks unprofessional on your business cards, social media bios, or email signatures. In 2026, YouTube has streamlined the process of claiming a "handle," which automatically creates your custom, easy-to-read URL.

In this guide, we’ll show you how to choose, claim, and change your custom YouTube handle and URL to better represent your brand.

## 1. Handles vs. Custom URLs: What’s the Difference?
By 2026, YouTube has replaced the old "Custom URL" system with **Handles**.
*   **A Handle** is your unique identifier (e.g., `@YourName`).
*   **Your Custom URL** is automatically generated based on your handle (e.g., `youtube.com/@YourName`).

The beauty of this system is that it’s consistent across the platform. Your handle is how people tag you in comments, mention you in Shorts, and find your profile page.

## 2. Eligibility Requirements
In the early days of YouTube, you needed 100 subscribers to get a custom URL. In 2026, these rules have changed to make it easier for new creators.

### Who Can Get a Handle?
Almost everyone! YouTube now allows new channels to choose a handle almost immediately after creation. However, your account must be:
*   **Verified via phone number** (in most regions).
*   **In good standing** (no active community guideline strikes).

## 3. How to Claim or Change Your Handle
You can do this from either your computer or your smartphone.

### On a Computer (YouTube Studio)
1.  Go to **YouTube Studio** (studio.youtube.com).
2.  On the left-hand menu, click on **Customization**.
3.  Click on the **Basic Info** tab.
4.  Under the **Handle** section, you will see your current handle. You can type in a new one here.
5.  If the handle is available, a green checkmark will appear.
6.  Click **Publish** at the top right.

### On a Mobile Device
1.  Open the YouTube app and tap your **Profile Picture**.
2.  Tap **Your Channel**.
3.  Tap the **Edit (pencil icon)** button.
4.  Tap on **Handle** to change it.

## 4. Tips for Choosing the Perfect Handle
Your handle is a permanent part of your brand. Don't rush the decision.

### Keep it Short and Memorable
Avoid using long strings of numbers (e.g., `@CookingMaster199827`). If `@CookingMaster` is taken, try `@Chef[Name]` or `@CookWith[Name]`.

### Consistency Across Social Media
Ideally, your YouTube handle should be the same as your Instagram, TikTok, and Twitter handles. This makes it much easier for your fans to find you on every platform.

### Avoid Special Characters
While you can use underscores or dots, it’s better to stick to plain letters. It’s easier for people to type and less likely to lead to errors.

## 5. What Happens to Your Old URL?
If you are an established creator who already had an old custom URL (e.g., `youtube.com/c/OldName`), don't worry. In 2026, YouTube automatically redirects your old URLs to your new `@handle` URL. You won't lose any traffic from old links you’ve posted around the web.

## 7. Troubleshooting Common Issues
*   **"Handle is Unavailable":** This means someone else has already claimed it. YouTube handles are unique. If you have a trademarked brand name and someone else is using it, you can file a trademark infringement claim through YouTube’s legal help center.
*   **"I Can't Change It":** YouTube typically limits how often you can change your handle (usually twice within a 14-day period). If you’ve reached the limit, you’ll have to wait.
*   **Handle vs. Channel Name:** Remember, your Channel Name and your Handle can be different. Your name can be "The Ultimate Cooking Show," while your handle is simply `@CookWithSam`.
