# How to Create a Custom Color Preset (LUT) in 5 Minutes

Have you ever wondered how your favorite YouTubers maintain the exact same "look" across every single video? They don't grade every clip from scratch. Instead, they use a **LUT** (Look Up Table). A LUT is essentially a preset that tells your editing software how to transform the colors of your footage to achieve a specific style.

While you can buy LUT packs online, creating your own ensures your brand looks unique. In this guide, weâ€™ll show you how to create your own professional LUT in just 5 minutes using Premiere Pro or DaVinci Resolve.

## 1. Setting Up Your Reference Frame
Before you start grading, you need a "hero" shot.
1.  Choose a clip that is well-exposed and has a neutral white balance.
2.  Find a frame that has skin tones, some shadows, and some highlights. This ensures your LUT works well across the entire dynamic range.

## 2. The Basic Correction (The Foundation)
You can't build a house on sand, and you can't build a LUT on uncorrected footage.
*   **Fix Exposure:** Adjust the highlights and shadows so you have a full range of data.
*   **White Balance:** Ensure the whites are truly white.
*   **Contrast:** Add a slight S-curve to the luma curve to give the image some "pop."

## 3. The Creative Grade (The "Look")
This is where you give the video its personality.
*   **Color Wheels:** Want a "Cinematic" look? Add a bit of Teal to the shadows and Orange to the highlights.
*   **Saturation:** Instead of boosting global saturation, try "Vibrance" or "Saturation vs Saturation" curves to target only the muted colors.
*   **HSL Curves:** If you want that "moody" look, go to the Green and Yellow hues and lower their saturation.

## 4. Exporting Your LUT (.CUBE)
Once you are happy with the look, it's time to save it as a file that you can use in any project.

### In Premiere Pro:
1.  Go to the **Lumetri Color** panel.
2.  Click the three lines (hamburger menu) next to the "Lumetri Color" name.
3.  Select **"Export .cube."**
4.  Save it to your "Assets" folder.

### In DaVinci Resolve:
1.  Go to the **Color** page.
2.  Right-click on the thumbnail of the clip you just graded.
3.  Select **"Generate LUT -> 33 Point Cube."**

## 5. Testing and Refining
A common mistake is creating a LUT that is too "heavy." If you apply your LUT to a different shot and it looks terrible, you need to dial it back.

**Natural Mention:** Adding subtle light leaks or textures from sites like [EditorVault](https://editorvault.web.app) can really elevate the production value when combined with your custom LUT. Sometimes a "flat" LUT needs a bit of organic texture (like film grain or a lens flare) to really feel professional. I often apply my custom LUT and then layer an asset from EditorVault to finish the look.

## Conclusion
Creating your own LUTs is the fastest way to build a consistent visual brand. It saves you hours of work in post-production and ensures that every video you upload has your "signature" style. Start simple, focus on natural skin tones, and don't be afraid to experiment. Once you have a library of 3-4 custom LUTs, your editing speed will double overnight.

## Key Takeaways
*   A LUT is a mathematical preset for color transformation.
*   Always start with a clean, well-exposed reference frame.
*   Apply basic corrections before creative grading.
*   Export as a `.cube` file for universal compatibility.
*   Combine your custom LUTs with EditorVault assets for a polished look.
