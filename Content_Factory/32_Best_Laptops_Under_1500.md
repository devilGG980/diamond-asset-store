# Top 5 Best Laptops for Video Editing Under $1,500

Video editing is one of the most hardware-intensive tasks you can perform on a laptop. To edit 4K video smoothly, you need a powerful CPU, a dedicated GPU, at least 16GB of RAM, and a color-accurate screen. In the past, you had to spend $3,000+ to get this kind of performance.

In 2026, the "Mid-Range" market has exploded with high-performance machines that can handle professional edits without breaking the bank. Here are the top 5 best laptops for video editing under $1,500.

## 1. Apple MacBook Air (M3, 16GB RAM)
The MacBook Air with the M3 chip is a miracle of engineering. Because of Apple’s "Media Engine," it can edit 4K ProRes video better than many Windows laptops with dedicated graphics cards.
*   **Why it’s great:** Silent (no fans), incredible battery life, and the best trackpad in the industry.
*   **Note:** Make sure you upgrade to at least 16GB of RAM. The base 8GB model will struggle with complex 4K timelines.

## 2. ASUS ROG Zephyrus G14 (2024 Edition)
If you prefer Windows, the Zephyrus G14 is the gold standard for portable power. 
*   **Why it’s great:** It features a stunning OLED display which is perfect for color grading. It’s also one of the few laptops of this size that packs an NVIDIA RTX 40-series GPU, which accelerates your renders in Premiere and Resolve.
*   **Best For:** Editors who also like to game or who need a color-accurate screen for professional work.

## 3. Lenovo Legion Pro 5i
This is a "desktop replacement" laptop. It’s heavier than the others, but it offers the most raw power for the price.
*   **Why it’s great:** It has an incredible cooling system, meaning the CPU won't "throttle" (slow down) during long 4K exports. It also has plenty of ports, so you won't need to carry a dongle for your external hard drives.
*   **Best For:** Editors who work mostly from a desk and prioritize export speeds.

## 4. Acer Swift X 14
The Swift X is a "hidden gem" in the creator market. It’s thin, light, and looks like a standard office laptop, but it hides an NVIDIA GPU and an Intel Core i7 inside.
*   **Why it’s great:** It offers a high-resolution 2.8K OLED screen at a price point that is often hundreds of dollars cheaper than the competition.
*   **Best For:** Budget-conscious creators who want an OLED screen for the lowest possible price.

## 5. HP Envy 16
The Envy line is HP’s answer to the MacBook Pro. It features a large 16-inch screen, which is a massive advantage when you are looking at complex timelines with many tracks.
*   **Why it’s great:** The 16:10 aspect ratio gives you more vertical space for your timeline. It also has a very professional, minimal design that doesn't look like a "gaming" laptop.

## Conclusion
You don't need to spend $3,000 to be a professional video editor in 2026. Whether you choose the efficiency of the M3 MacBook Air or the raw power of the Lenovo Legion, all of these laptops are capable of producing high-end 4K content.
