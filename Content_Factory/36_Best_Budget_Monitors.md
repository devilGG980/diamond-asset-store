# Best Budget Monitors for Color Accurate Video Editing

One of the biggest mistakes new video editors make is grading their footage on a standard office monitor or a cheap laptop screen. If your monitor isn't "Color Accurate," you might spend hours making a video look perfect, only for it to look orange on an iPhone or too dark on a TV.

To be a professional, you need a monitor that covers a high percentage of the **sRGB** and **Rec.709** color gamuts. In 2026, you don't need a $5,000 Apple Studio Display to get this. Here are the best budget monitors for color-accurate video editing.

## 1. ASUS ProArt Display PA278QV
The ProArt line is designed specifically for creators. This 27-inch 1440p monitor comes "Calibrated Out of the Box," meaning it’s ready for professional work the moment you plug it in.
*   **Best Features:** 100% sRGB and 100% Rec.709 coverage. It also has a very flexible stand that allows for height, tilt, and pivot adjustments.
*   **Price:** Usually under $300.

## 2. BenQ PD2705Q
BenQ is a legendary name in the photography and video world. The PD series is their designer line, offering incredible color consistency.
*   **Best Features:** "Darkroom Mode," which adjusts brightness and contrast for work in dark environments, and a built-in KVM switch that allows you to control two computers with one monitor.
*   **Price:** Around $350.

## 3. Dell UltraSharp U2723QE
If you want 4K resolution on a budget, the UltraSharp series is the gold standard for office and creative work.
*   **Best Features:** It uses "IPS Black" technology, which offers 2x the contrast ratio of a standard IPS monitor. This makes your blacks look deeper and your colors look more "cinematic."
*   **Price:** Usually around $450-$500.

## 4. LG 27UP850N-W
This is the best value 4K monitor for Mac users because it includes USB-C Power Delivery. One cable can charge your MacBook while sending the video signal to the monitor.
*   **Best Features:** Hardware Calibration support and DCI-P3 95% coverage, which is excellent for social media and web content.
*   **Price:** Around $380.

## 5. ViewSonic VP2756-2K
ViewSonic’s "ColorPro" series is another great entry-level option for editors who prioritize color over resolution.
*   **Best Features:** Pantone Validated colors and a "Designer Mode" that optimizes the screen for CAD or animation work.
*   **Price:** Often found for under $250.
